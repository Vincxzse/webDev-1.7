How to Brighten Up Dark Underarms

Deodorants might contribute to darker underarms. Here's what could be causing it:

  1. Shaving

    Shaving leaves stubble that darkens underarms. Waxing removes hair from the root, preventing this.

  2. Dead Cells

    Dead cells can accumulate, darkening underarms. Exfoliate gently with a loofah or scrubs.

  3. Excessive Use of Deodorants

    Chemicals in deodorants can cause pigmentation. Use natural methods or deos for sensitive skin.

  4. Friction

    Tight clothes and excess weight cause friction, leading to darkening. Wear looser clothes and lose weight.

  5. Hereditary Factors

    Dark underarms can be hereditary due to weight gain, hormonal factors, or birth control. Consult a doctor.

  6. Diabetes

    Diabetes can cause pigmentation. Maintain insulin levels and use natural remedies.

Top 7 Ways To Get Rid Of Dark Underarms:

  1. Waxing or Electrolysis

    Waxing removes hair from the root and exfoliates. Electrolysis offers a permanent solution.

  2. Lemon Juice

    Lemon is a natural bleaching agent. Rub lemon daily before a bath and use moisturizer afterward.

  3. Lightening Masks

    Make a mask with gram flour, yogurt, lemon, and turmeric. Use for 15-20 minutes and wash off.

  4. Saffron Mixture

    Mix saffron in milk or cream and apply overnight to lighten underarms and kill bacteria.

  5. Less Usage of Deodorants

    Reduce deodorant use. Use baking soda, alum, or anti-fungal powders as alternatives.

  6. Sandalwood and Rose Water

    Make a paste of sandalwood and rose water. Use daily to lighten and cool skin.

  7. Whitening Scrubs

    If dark underarms are due to dead cells, use whitening scrubs suitable for sensitive skin.
